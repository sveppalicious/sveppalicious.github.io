| 13  | Otidiformes         | From Greek *ōtis* / Latin *Otis* (“bustard”)                                                                                               | “bustard-like”                       | High       | https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/otis                                                                      |
| 14  | Musophagiformes     | From *Musophaga*, usually interpreted as Greek *Musa* (banana/plantain, in scientific usage) + *-phagos* (“eating”)                        | “banana-eater” / “plantain-eater”    | Medium     | https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/Musophaga                                                     |
| 15  | Cuculiformes        | From Latin *cuculus* (“cuckoo”)                                                                                                            | “cuckoo-like”                        | High       | https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/cuculus                                                                   |
| 16  | Opisthocomiformes   | From Greek *opistho-* (“behind, at the back”) + *komē* (“hair”), via *Opisthocomus*; refers to the hoatzin’s crest                         | “back-haired” / “long-haired behind” | High       | https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/opisthocome                                                   |
| 17  | Gruiformes          | From Latin *grus* (“crane”)                                                                                                                | “crane-like”                         | High       | https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/Grus                                                          |
| 18  | Charadriiformes     | From Greek *kharadrios*, a bird associated with ravines or river valleys, from *kharadra* (“ravine”, “torrent-bed”), via *Charadrius*      | “ravine-bird-like”                   | Medium     | https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/Charadrius                                                    |
| 19  | Caprimulgiformes    | From Latin *capra* (“goat”) + *mulgere* (“to milk”), via *Caprimulgus*; based on the old myth that nightjars suck goats’ milk              | “goat-milker” / “goatsucker”         | High       | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caprimulgus                                                                |
| 20  | Nyctibiiformes      | From Greek *nyx/nykt-* (“night”) + *bios* (“life”), via *Nyctibius*                                                                        | “night-living”                       | Medium     | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nyctibius                                                                  |
| 21  | Steatornithiformes  | From Greek *stear/stearatos* (“fat, tallow”) + *ornis* (“bird”), via *Steatornis*                                                          | “fat-bird”                           | High       | https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/Steatornis                                                    |
| 22  | Podargiformes       | From Greek *podargos*; often glossed as “swift-footed” (sometimes interpreted differently in classical usage)                              | “swift-footed”                       | Medium     | https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/Podargus                                                      |
| 23  | Aegotheliformes     | From Greek *aix/aigos* (“goat”) + *thēlazō* (“to suckle”), via *Aegotheles*                                                                | “goat-sucker”                        | High       | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aegotheles                                                                 |
| 24  | Apodiformes         | From Greek *apous/apod-* (“without feet”, “footless”), referring to the tiny feet of swifts                                                | “footless”                           | High       | https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/Apodi ; https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/-iformes   |
| 25  | Phaethontiformes    | From Greek *Phaethon* (“shining, radiant”), also the mythological Phaethon                                                                 | “radiant” / “shining”                | Medium     | https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/Pha%C3%ABthon ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phaethon_(bird) |
| 26  | Eurypygiformes      | From Greek *eurys* (“broad”) + *pygē* (“rump, buttocks”), via *Eurypyga*                                                                   | “broad-rumped”                       | Medium     | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurypyga                                                                   |
| 27  | Gaviiformes         | From Latin *gavia*, an old bird name later applied to loons/divers                                                                         | “loon-like”                          | Medium     | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gavia                                                                      |
| 28  | Sphenisciformes     | From Greek *spheniskos* (“small wedge”); the exact semantic path is old and a bit obscure                                                  | “wedge-shaped”                       | High       | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spheniscus                                                                 |
| 29  | Procellariiformes   | From Latin *procella* (“storm”), via *Procellaria*                                                                                         | “storm-bird-like”                    | High       | https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/Procellaria                                                   |
| 30  | Ciconiiformes       | From Latin *ciconia* (“stork”)                                                                                                             | “stork-like”                         | High       | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ciconia                                                                    |
| 31  | Suliformes          | From genus *Sula*, from Old Norse/*súla* and Icelandic *súla*, the word for a gannet                                                       | “gannet-like” / booby-gannet-like    | Medium     | https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/Sula                                                          |
| 32  | Pelecaniformes      | From Greek *pelekan*, from *pelekys* (“axe”), via *Pelecanus*; probably referring to the bill shape                                        | “axe-billed” / pelican-like          | High       | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pelican                                                                    |
| 33  | Accipitriformes     | From Latin *accipiter* (“hawk”); probably connected with the idea of swift or pointed wings, though the deeper etymology is debated        | “hawk-like”                          | High       | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accipiter ; https://www.etymonline.com/word/accipiter                      |
| 34  | Strigiformes        | From Latin *strix* (“owl”, especially screech owl)                                                                                         | “owl-like”                           | High       | https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/Strix ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strix_(bird)            |
| 35  | Coliiformes         | From genus *Colius*; deeper origin uncertain                                                                                               | “mousebird-like”                     | Low        | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colius                                                                     |
| 36  | Leptosomiformes     | From Greek *leptos* (“slender, thin”) + *sōma* (“body”), via *Leptosomus*                                                                  | “slender-bodied”                     | Medium     | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leptosomus                                                                 |
| 37  | Trogoniformes       | From Greek *trōgōn* (“nibbling”, “gnawing”); refers to trogons gnawing nest holes in rotten wood                                           | “nibbling”                           | High       | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trogon                                                                     |
| 38  | Bucerotiformes      | From Greek *bous* (“ox”) + *keras* (“horn”), via *Buceros*                                                                                 | “ox-horned”                          | High       | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buceros                                                                    |
| 39  | Piciformes          | From Latin *picus* (“woodpecker”)                                                                                                          | “woodpecker-like”                    | High       | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Picus_(bird)                                                               |
| 40  | Coraciiformes       | From Greek *korakias*, from *korax* (“raven”, “crow”), via *Coracias*                                                                      | “raven-like”                         | High       | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coracias                                                                   |
| 41  | Cariamiformes       | From *Cariama*, ultimately from a Tupi-form word such as *çariama*, usually glossed as “crested”                                           | “crested one” / seriema-like         | High       | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seriema                                                                    |
| 42  | Falconiformes       | From Late Latin *falco* (“falcon”), from Latin *falx, falcis* (“sickle”), referring to the bird’s claws or hooked bill                     | “sickle-clawed” / falcon-like        | High       | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Falcon ; https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Falco                              |
| 43  | Psittaciformes      | From Latin *psittacus* (“parrot”), from Greek *psittakos*                                                                                  | “parrot-like”                        | High       | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psittacus                                                                  |
| 44  | Passeriformes       | From Latin *passer* (“sparrow”)                                                                                                            | “sparrow-like”                       | High       | https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/passerine ; https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/passer             |
